\begin{frame}{Problem 1}
Let $a$ and $b$ be five-digit palindromes 
(without leading zeroes) such that $a<b$
and there are no other
five-digit palindromes strictly between $a$ and $b$.
What are all possible values of $b-a$? 
(A number is a palindrome if it reads 
the same forwards and backwards in base 10.)
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Answer 1}
Casework on how many `carries' there are between consecutives.
$100$, $110$, $11$.\\
Source: HMMT Feb 2018 C2.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Problem 2}
Quadrilateral $ABCD$ is cyclic with $AB = CD = 6$. Given that $AC = BD = 8$ and $AD+3 = BC$, what is $[ABCD]?$\\
\begin{block}{Note}
$[A_1\dots A_n]$ denotes the area of that polygon.
\end{block}
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 2}
Ptolemy to find the missing lengths $AD$, $BC$, then Brahmagupta to find the area. $33\sqrt{15}/4$.
\\
Source: BMT 2020 TB G2.
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Problem 3}
Every face of a cube is colored one of $3$ colors at random. What is the expected number of edges
that lie along two faces of different colors?
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 3}
Linearity of expectation. Probability that two adjacent faces are different colours is $2/3$. $8$.
\\
Source: BMT 2018 TB C1.

\begin{frame}{Problem 7}
Right triangle $ABC$ with its right angle at $B$ has angle bisector $\ol{AD}$ 
with $D$ on $\ol{BC}$, as well
as altitude $\ol{BE}$ with $E$ on $\ol{AC}$. 
If $\ol{DE} \perp\ol{BC}$ and $AB = 1$, compute $AC$.
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 7}
Trig bash. $(1+\sqrt5)/2$.\\
Source: BMT 2021 TB G2
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Problem 8}
Let $\Omega$ and $\omega$ be circles with radii 123 and 61, respectively, 
such that the center of $\Omega$ lies on $\omega$. 
A chord of $\Omega$ is cut by $\omega$ into three segments, 
whose lengths are in the ratio $1 : 2 : 3$ in that order. 
Given that
this chord is not a diameter of $\omega$, compute the length of this chord.
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 8}
Synthetic and power of a point. $42$.\\
Source: 2024 HMMT Feb G3
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Problem 9}

\begin{frame}{Problem 11}
Greta writes $2, 3, . . . , 101$ on a chalkboard. 
Every minute she erases two chosen numbers $x,y$ from the board,
and writes $xy/(x+y-1)$ in their place. 
After 99 minutes, what number remains?
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 11}
This is a contrived invariant problem. The invariant in question is 
\[\prod_k \frac1{1-1/x_k}\]
where the nums on the board are $x_1,...$.\\
Source: BMT 2022 TB A3
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Problem 12}
A positive integer is called extra-even if all of its digits are even. Compute the number of positive
integers n less than or equal to 2022 such that both n and 2n are both extra-even.
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer 12}
Figure out from experimenting that $0,2,4$ are the only legal digits. $31$.\\
Source: BMT 2022 TB C2
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Problem}
Let a and b be real numbers such that $a + b = 16$ and $a^3 - b^3 = ab^2 - a^2b + 2048$. 
Compute $ab^2$.
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Answer}
Factor, divide, etc to obtain $a,b$. $192$.\\
Source: BMT 2025 TB A2
